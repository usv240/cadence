# Cadence Competitive Landscape

Last reviewed: July 20, 2026

Cadence should not be described as replacing AAC assessment, established AAC software, dedicated eye-tracking hardware, voice banking, or atypical-speech recognition. It is an early, complementary live-conversation prototype.

| Category | Public example or source | What it does | Cadence's differentiated focus |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Established AAC systems | [ASHA AAC Practice Portal](https://www.asha.org/practice-portal/professional-issues/augmentative-and-alternative-communication/) | AAC covers a broad range of communication methods and should be fitted to a person's needs, partners, and environment. | Stages a few response options from the current conversation while preserving user choice. |
| Predictive text research | [SpeakFaster, Nature Communications](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-53873-3) | Studies language-model aided text entry and motor-action savings for AAC users. | Extends the idea of multiple candidate choices into a live conversational flow: captions, reply staging, repair, reactions, floor-holding, and initiation. This is a design distinction, not a comparative performance claim. |
| Personal voice and live speech | [Apple Accessibility](https://www.apple.com/accessibility/) | Provides Personal Voice and Live Speech for voice preservation and typed speech output. | Uses selectable text-to-speech and an optional wording-style card. Cadence does not offer voice cloning and does not replace voice preservation tools. |
| Atypical-speech recognition | [Voiceitt](https://voiceitt.com/) | Recognizes an individual's non-standard speech for communication, dictation, and captions. | Listens to the surrounding conversation and can expand a short user-provided idea. This can complement, rather than substitute for, personalized speech recognition. |
| Dedicated eye-gaze AAC | [Tobii Dynavox product overview](https://downloads.tobiidynavox.com/Other/Tradeshow/Event-Product-Brochure_Letter_TD_en-US.pdf) | Provides established, comprehensive AAC solutions with eye gaze, touch, and switch access. | Offers an experimental local-camera focus aid with calibration, speed choice, clear off control, and explicit confirmation. It is not a replacement for dedicated eye tracking. |

## Claim boundaries

- Do not say Cadence is better than a named product without a comparable independent study.
- Do not reuse competitor copy, datasets, figures, logos, or branding.
- Link to original sources for facts and distinguish design inspiration from verified product outcomes.
- Treat accessibility and clinical suitability as user-specific; recommend AAC and speech-language professional input where appropriate.
- Report internal metrics as product telemetry only, never as clinical effectiveness or a head-to-head benchmark.
