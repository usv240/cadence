# Cadence Beta Tester Outreach

## Email to a community coordinator or clinician

**Subject options:**

- `Could I ask your community for 5 minutes of feedback on an AAC prototype?`
- `Seeking respectful feedback on an early ALS communication tool`
- `Would this be appropriate to share with your ALS/AAC community?`

Hi [Name],

I’m a North Carolina developer building **Cadence**, an early web tool intended to help people with ALS and AAC users stay in a fast conversation. When someone speaks, it prepares a few possible replies; the user chooses what, if anything, is spoken.

I’m not asking for an endorsement or for any member contact information. I’m hoping to learn from **5-10 volunteers** and would be grateful if you could tell me whether it would be appropriate to share this opt-in invitation with your community, or point me to the right SLP, assistive-technology professional, or group coordinator.

It is a prototype - not a medical device or emergency tool. Testing takes about 5-10 minutes, needs no video call, and asks people not to share private health information or conversation transcripts.

Cadence: **https://cadence-orcin-kappa.vercel.app**
Feedback form: **https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSf8oLHFO0OgDguwSNLFaodKFK7I5h0Zkjxf-BZBuNm08nE7bg/viewform?usp=publish-editor**

I’ve attached a one-page quick-start guide. Honest feedback about what feels useful, confusing, slow, or unsafe would directly shape the next version.

Thank you for the work you do for this community,

[Your name]
[Optional phone or email]

## Short invitation a coordinator can forward

**Subject:** `Help test an early conversation-support tool for AAC users`

Cadence is an early web prototype that listens to a live conversation and prepares a few user-controlled replies to speak with one tap. It is looking for brief feedback from people living with ALS, AAC users, caregivers, and speech-language or assistive-technology professionals.

Try it here: **https://cadence-orcin-kappa.vercel.app**
Share feedback here: **https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSf8oLHFO0OgDguwSNLFaodKFK7I5h0Zkjxf-BZBuNm08nE7bg/viewform?usp=publish-editor**

It is free, optional, and takes about 5-10 minutes. Please do not use it for emergencies or submit private health information, names, or conversation transcripts.

## Google Form: suggested questions

Keep the form short. Make every question optional except consent to submit feedback.

1. **Which best describes you?**
   - Person who uses AAC
   - Person living with ALS
   - Family member or caregiver
   - Speech-language professional / assistive technology professional
   - Other

2. **What device and browser did you use?**
   - Short answer

3. **What did you try?**
   - Listen/captions
   - Choosing a reply
   - Starting a conversation
   - My needs / quick phrases
   - Scanning mode
   - Instant device voice or OpenAI voice
   - Other

4. **What felt useful or promising?**
   - Paragraph answer

5. **What felt confusing, slow, inaccurate, or unsafe?**
   - Paragraph answer

6. **Did the reply choices feel connected to what was just said?**
   - Yes / Sometimes / No / I did not try this

7. **Would you feel comfortable using this in a real conversation? Why or why not?**
   - Paragraph answer

8. **What is the one thing you would change first?**
   - Paragraph answer

9. **May I contact you for one follow-up question?**
   - Yes, with this email (optional)
   - No

10. **Consent**
    - “I understand this is an early prototype, not an emergency or medical tool, and I am choosing to share feedback.”
    - Required checkbox

## Before you send

- Attach `output/pdf/cadence-quick-start.pdf` to the email.
- Verify the Google Form opens in an incognito browser before sending.
- Do not ask people to send recordings, full transcripts, health information, API keys, or passwords.
- Ask group moderators or clinic/support-group coordinators for permission before posting to their communities.
